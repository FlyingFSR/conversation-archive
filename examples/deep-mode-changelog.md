# Changelog

## Part 1: Decisions & Cognitive Shifts

## 2025-02-21 ｜ Loyalty and Leaving Can Coexist
**Shift**: Moved from framing the career decision as "loyal (stay) vs. selfish (leave)" to recognizing both can be true simultaneously. The guilt didn't disappear, but it stopped being a veto.
**Context**: Had been stuck for weeks on "how can I leave people who count on me?" Breakthrough came when reframing: caring about the team is real, AND wanting to leave is real. They don't cancel each other out. "I kept waiting for the guilt to go away so I could decide. Maybe the guilt doesn't have to go away. Maybe I just decide with it still there."

## 2025-02-07 ｜ Research as Avoidance, Not Preparation
**Shift**: Recognized that his pattern of exhaustive research before decisions wasn't thoroughness — it was a way to avoid the moment of actually deciding.
**Context**: Had spent three weeks reading about career transitions, building spreadsheets, comparing options. When asked "what would you need to see in the research to finally decide?" he paused for a long time and said: "I think the research is me avoiding the scary part, not preparing for it." First time naming this pattern.

## 2025-01-24 ｜ First Time Saying "I Want to Leave" Out Loud
**Decision**: Said "I want to leave" for the first time in any context, and noticed his own reaction was relief rather than fear.
**Context**: Had been describing work frustrations in abstract, systemic terms. When asked to just say what he wanted — not what made sense, not what was fair to others — he said it and then went quiet. "I've been thinking it for months but hearing myself say it... it's different."
