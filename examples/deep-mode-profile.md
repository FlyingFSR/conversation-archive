## Core Themes

### Career Transition
- **Current state**: Has moved from "should I leave?" to actively planning the exit. The decision itself feels settled — what remains is logistics and timing. Still navigating guilt about leaving a team that depends on him, but beginning to separate "loyalty to people" from "obligation to stay." In his words: "I think I've known for months. I just needed to stop pretending the knowing wasn't there."
- **Key trajectory**: Frustration without naming it (01-10) → First time saying "I want to leave" out loud (01-24) → Identified guilt as the real blocker, not logistics (02-07) → Reframed loyalty as "I can care about them AND leave" (02-21) → Actively planning timeline (03-11) → Decision feels settled; focus shifted to execution (03-18)
- **Connections**: Links to Identity & Self-Worth (leaving feels like failing), Communication Patterns (hasn't told his manager yet)

### Identity & Self-Worth
- **Current state**: Starting to disentangle professional identity from job title. Previously, "I'm a senior engineer at [Company]" was load-bearing — it answered "am I successful?" Now experimenting with other answers, though none feel as solid yet. The gap is uncomfortable but he's learning to sit with it rather than rush to fill it.
- **Key trajectory**: "I am my job title" (01-10) → Noticed the equation for the first time (01-24) → "What else am I?" as genuine question, not rhetorical (02-14) → Sitting with the discomfort of not knowing (03-04)
- **Connections**: Career Transition (leaving removes the identity anchor), Communication Patterns (how he introduces himself)

## Patterns & Traits

- **Over-preparation as anxiety management**: When facing uncertain situations, tends to research exhaustively rather than act. Recognizes this but frames it as "being thorough." Breakthrough on 02-07: "I think the research is me avoiding the scary part, not preparing for it." (02-07)
- **Guilt-driven decision-making**: Defaults to "what would disappoint the fewest people?" rather than "what do I actually want?" Slowly building awareness of this pattern. (01-24)
- **Thinks in systems**: Naturally maps relationships and dynamics into mental models. This is a strength in analysis but sometimes distances him from his own emotions — "I can diagram why I feel this way but I still feel it." (02-14)

## Effective Strategies

- **The "say it out loud" test**: Saying a tentative decision out loud to another person (or AI) and noticing his own body's reaction. First used 01-24 when he said "I want to leave" and realized he felt relief, not fear. Has become a reliable signal. (01-24)
- **Separating "both true" from "choose one"**: When facing guilt vs. desire, reframing from "which one wins?" to "both are true — now what?" Reduced paralysis around the career decision. (02-21)
